Residence permit


The first and the most important step after coming to Denmark!





What is a residence permit?


Is a document or card required in some countries, allowing a foreign national to reside in a country for a fixed or indefinite length of time.


If you are EU/ EEA or Swiss citizen anticipate that your stay in Denmark will have a duration of more than three months, you must apply for an EU residence document before the end of the three months.


If you are from a non-EU/EEA country, you must have applied and been issued with a residence permit and a work permit before you can start working in Denmark.


If you change your basis for residence during your stay in Denmark – e.g. from being a student to becoming an employee – you must apply for a new residence document.The same applies if you are a student and change from one university-level course to another


When?


We recommend you to apply as soon as possible. Of course school provide a day when you can apply for it but If you came earlier then a 15th of august you shouldn´t wait for it but directly go there and apply. It usually takes 3-4 weeks to get it in August, September coming.


Where?


In Aarhus there is only one office where you can go : Statsforvaltningen Afdeling Aarhus Lyseng Allé 1 8270 Højbjerg



Opening hours


Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday: 08.00-14.00
Thursday: 12.00-16.00
Friday: 08.00-14.00



You will need:


• Completed Application for EU-residence (OD1 form)

• Study documentation

• Passport / National ID card

• Copy of Passport or Identity Card

• Passport size photo (two if you are not an EU/EEA national) -printed on photo paper! photo requirements


When you have received your EU residence document, you will be able to apply to the Citizen Service in your local authority to obtain a civil registration number (CPR number), register your address and obtain a medical card. As a national of a non-Nordic country, you cannot be issued with a CPR number without having a residence document.
