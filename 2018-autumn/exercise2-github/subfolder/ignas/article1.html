Different career options for multimedia designers

Source

So, you have finally decided to become a multimedia designer. Awesome, it’s an interesting profession with a lot freedom and ways to show your creativity. One of the main problems that students have is not specializing in a certain place. In this article, I'll look at some areas where you can develop in.

1.Front End Development

Your website is a sum of layers—structure, data, design, content, and functionality. Creating that user-facing functionality is the job of a front-end developer. Using a combination of mark up languages, design, and client-side scripts and frameworks, the front-end developer creates the environment for everything that users see and touch: content, layout, and interaction.

Generally, a front-end web developer’s services include:

  • Bringing a designer’s concept to life with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Implementing responsive design for websites
  • Production, modification, and maintenance of websites and web application user interfaces
  • Testing the site during development for usability and fixing any bugs
  • And more…

2.User Experience (UX) Design

User experience (abbreviated as UX) is how a person feels when they are interacting with a system, whether it’s a website, web application or desktop software. UX designers study and evaluate how users feel about and use a system, looking at things like ease of use, perception of the value, utility, and efficiency in performing tasks. They crunch data, organize information, understand human behaviour as it relates to their projects, and create interactive experiences that draw users in and keep them coming back. Great UX designers think so that their users don’t have to.

Generally, a user experience designer’s services include:

  • Collecting information about the product/studying target audience
  • Creating prototypes
  • Testing products
  • Creating/organizing product structure
  • And more…

3.Content creator

A content creator is a person who creates original content for usage mostly(but not limited) on online platforms. They usually target a specific end-user/audience in specific contexts.

Generally, a content creator’s services include:

  • Writing blog posts
  • Designing graphics
  • Editing videos
  • Creating offline content such as brochures
  • And more...
